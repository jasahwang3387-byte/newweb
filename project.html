üèõ Institutional Framework & Key Agencies

Disaster Management is governed by the *Disaster Management Act, 2005*, establishing a tiered structure for response.

National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA)

Apex body for laying down policies, plans, and national guidelines. Oversees the National Disaster Response Force (NDRF).
Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MoRTH)

Central Ministry responsible for the sector-specific *Disaster Management Plan (DMP), focusing on restoring connectivity and applying the **'Build Back Better'* principle.
National Disaster Response Force (NDRF)

*Specialised response force* for search, rescue, and relief. Deploys quick reaction teams for road clearance post-disaster.

üìã The 4R Strategy for Road Disaster Management

A continuous and integrated process covering the disaster management cycle:

1. Risk Reduction & Prevention

Implementing disaster-resilient standards, rectifying accident-prone *"Black Spots"* on NHs, and enforcing stricter vehicle safety features.

2. Preparedness

*Vulnerability Mapping* of bridges/stretches, *Pre-positioning* of heavy machinery (excavators, temporary bridges), and using Early Warning Systems (IMD/CWC).

3. Response

Rapid activation and deployment of NDRF. Priority on *immediate restoration of traffic, clearing debris, plugging breaches, and providing **Emergency Care*.

4. Recovery & Restoration

Swift damage assessment followed by reconstruction. Applying the *'Build Back Better' (BBB)* principle for long-term resilience.
